## THE ONE SENTENCE

Careified is to home care what Uber was to taxis and Airbnb was 
to hotels — it didn't improve the old system, it replaced it.

---

## WHY THIS EXISTS

The home care market has a reliability and identity crisis on both sides.

Caregivers are treated as gig workers — disposable, invisible, 
with no portable reputation. Every new agency means rebuilding 
from scratch. Their professional identity is owned by whoever 
employed them last — not by them.

Agencies are stuck in a broken loop:
Post on Indeed → receive 200 unvetted resumes → manually screen 
→ call references one by one → caregiver disappears before you 
move fast enough → repeat.

If an agency can't reach a caregiver fast enough, they're gone.
Caregivers wait for whoever pays more, is closest, and easiest.
There is no reliability. No continuity. No reputation system.

Nobody was fixing this properly. That's the gap.

## THE VISION

Careified is the operating system for home care.

Not a job board. Not a staffing app. Not a resume database.
The infrastructure caregiving never had.

Three layers:

LAYER 1 — REPUTATION
Caregivers own their professional identity forever.
One verified profile that travels with them across their career.
Reputation compounds instead of resetting.

LAYER 2 — OPERATIONS  
Agencies run their entire operation from one platform.
Recruiting, rostering, scheduling, family communication, 
retention — all connected, all visible, all actionable.

LAYER 3 — AI INTELLIGENCE
AI that works across WhatsApp, Telegram, and SMS.
Coordinators run their day from a chat window.
Caregivers get a career assistant in their pocket.
Families get real-time updates without downloading anything.
The platform thinks ahead so humans don't have to.

## THE PRODUCT PRINCIPLES

### 1. Replace, don't improve
We are not building a better Indeed.
We are making Indeed irrelevant for home care.
Every feature should make the old way feel embarrassing.

### 2. AI is the product, not a feature
The AI assistant over WhatsApp/Telegram/SMS is not a 
nice-to-have. It is the reason agencies never leave.
Click-click-enter-done. That is the experience standard.

### 3. The caregiver owns their reputation
Not the agency. Not Careified. The caregiver.
When they leave an agency their profile comes with them.
That portability is the supply-side moat.

### 4. Trust is built in layers
Free users see basic info.
Verified users see more.
Agencies see full profiles.
Families see only what's relevant to their loved one.
Every layer is earned, not assumed.

### 5. The platform thinks ahead
Credential expiring? Platform flags it before anyone asks.
Caregiver running late? Platform notifies before family worries.
Schedule conflict? Platform surfaces it before placement fails.
Proactive > reactive. Always.

### 6. Both sides pay, both sides win
Agencies pay for efficiency. Caregivers pay for visibility.
Neither side should feel like they're subsidising the other.
Value must be obvious to both within the first session.

## THE AI VISION — IN DETAIL

### Agency AI Assistant (WhatsApp/Telegram/SMS)
Coordinator messages: "Find me a PSW in Scarborough, 
Tagalog speaking, available Monday mornings"
AI returns top 3 matches with scores.
Coordinator replies: "Shortlist all three, call Maria's references"
AI does it. No clicking. No navigating. No training required.

### Caregiver AI Assistant (WhatsApp)
"I'm running 15 minutes late to Mrs. Johnson"
→ AI notifies family and agency automatically

"Are there new matches for me this week?"
→ AI returns opportunities with details

"Remind me when my VSC expires"
→ AI sets reminder, sends 30-day notice

### Family AI Check-ins (Outbound calls + WhatsApp)
AI calls family: "Does Maria's Monday 9am still work?"
Family says no, needs 10am.
AI updates schedule, notifies caregiver and agency.
All stakeholders informed. Zero coordinator time spent.

### AIRecruit (Outbound screening + references)
AI calls caregivers, screens them, scores them.
AI calls references, structured interview, flags concerns.
AI calls placed caregivers at 2 weeks: "How is it going?"
Flags early churn signals before caregiver disappears.

## THE AGENCY ROSTER — KEY INSIGHT

Agencies already maintain caregiver data manually.
Instead of asking caregivers to build profiles 
(friction) — agencies seed the supply for us.

Agency uploads their caregiver roster → 
Careified creates stub profiles →
Caregiver gets notified "claim your profile" →
Caregiver owns it forever, even after leaving

This is the viral loop that builds the supply side 
without a separate caregiver acquisition campaign.

Feature name: Agency Roster
Launch target: June 15 2026
